Pretzels, a Snack With a Twist

Walk into any store and you'll be presented with pretzels in every flavor shape and size,

but what makes this treat stand out from the rest? Beside the obvious pretzel shape, there

is at least one major difference this staple snack has over the rest, its flexibility. Pretzels

can be hard, soft, chocolate dipped, cinnamon sprayed, and so much more. The biggest difference

is between the soft and hard pretzel variants. This major difference in texutre, makes an even

larger change on a consumer's diet. According to an article featured on healthline.com, hard

pretzels outmatch soft pretzels in nearly every nutritional matchup. Below is a graph presenting

the basice nutional difference between soft and hard pretzels:


1 soft pretzel with salt(120 grams) 1.5 ounces of hard pretzels (42.5 grams)
Calories 408 163
Fat (grams) 4 1
Sodium (percentage) 41% daily value 23% daily value

The graph presents some disturbing truths about our soft friends. They contain more calories, fat, and close

to half of the recommended daily sodium intake. Overall they seem like a worse choice on every level except for

texture. EatingWell.com would argue against that conclusion, as they make a compelling argument for the pretzels'

softer younger brother. They say that pretzels can be used to dip into protein rich or fat rich substances to

make up for the added calories. This valid argument turns the battle into more of a stalemate, as the choice

becomes less about pure calories, and more about how they are being treated.
